Whereby

Identifying & solving customer pain points

Whereby provides video meetings for consumers and organisations, much like Zoom or Google Hangouts.

Jul '22-Nov '22

Due to a lack of customer feedback for a new product called Embedded, I conducted a customer journey mapping exercise - leading several workshops to gain insight from the Customer Success Managers.

Use cases, pain points and potential remedies were captured and presented to the business with the aim of increasing 'activation' in the Embedded product.

On the Meetings product, patterns in the customer feedback identified problems surrounding hosting and scheduling meetings.

I delivered designs for a BETA programme and a commercial release of 'Flex Rooms', a new feature that allowed customers to host meetings without being present.

I pitched a new scheduling feature to the business, designed to help customers grow their business without any coding skills.

I also delivered three smaller projects to ensure users were extracting as much value as possible from the products: upgrades, shared room promotion and a unified subscription page.

The user research phase revealed key insights about customer behavior patterns and pain points that directly informed our design decisions and feature prioritization.

Wireframes and prototypes were created to test different interaction patterns and validate our design assumptions with real users before moving to high-fidelity designs.

The final UI designs incorporated the feedback from user testing sessions, resulting in a more intuitive and accessible interface for all user types.

A/B testing was conducted on key user flows to measure the impact of design changes and ensure we were moving in the right direction with our solutions.

Post-launch metrics showed significant improvements in user engagement and conversion rates, validating the design decisions made throughout the project.

The project's success led to increased user satisfaction scores and positive feedback from both internal stakeholders and end users.

The lessons learned from this project have been documented and shared with the broader design team to inform future projects and maintain consistency across products.
